About Me

Hi, I am currently an assistant professor of hydroclimatology in the School of Geography and Planning at Sun Yat-sen University (SYSU). I obtained my PhD in hydrology and climate science from The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) in 2022, under the supervision of Prof. Shuo Wang. Before joining SYSU, I was a postdoctoral researcher in the Hydro-Climate Extremes Research Group led by Prof. Louise Slater at the University of Oxford.

My research focuses on extreme weather and climate events, machine learning-based flood prediction, climate change impacts, multivariate statistics, including copulas and pair-copula constructions, and hydrological modelling. I use machine learning methods and climate model outputs to improve global flood prediction and projection. I also develop and apply multivariate probabilistic approaches to assess the characteristics, hazards, and risks of hydroclimate extremes under climate change. In addition, I use climate simulations and hydrological models to investigate future changes in concurrent and consecutive water-related hazards in a warming climate.

Feel free to get in touch and share your ideas by email.
