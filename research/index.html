Advancing Hydroclimate Science for a Resilient Future

Research Directions

Our research focuses on understanding, predicting, and assessing hydroclimate extremes in a warming climate. We combine climate observations, model simulations, hydrological modelling, machine learning, and probabilistic risk analysis to study floods, droughts, heatwaves, and compound water-related hazards across regions and scales.

Compound Events

We study how and why compound events, including concurrent droughts and heatwaves as well as dry-wet abrupt alternation, are changing in a warming climate and what those changes mean for ecosystems, infrastructure, and society.

Climate Extremes and Inequality

Climate extremes do not affect all communities equally. We study how floods, droughts, heatwaves, and abrupt hydroclimatic shifts interact with socioeconomic vulnerability, exposure, and adaptation capacity, with the goal of understanding who is most affected by emerging climate risks and why.

Multivariate Assessment of Hydrologic Extremes

We investigate how floods and droughts are changing under climate change. Our work quantifies their frequency, intensity, spatial patterns, dependence structures, and socioeconomic impacts, with a focus on risk assessment and future projections.

Hydrological Forecasting

We develop data-driven and process-based approaches for hydrological forecasting across river basins and larger spatial scales. This work integrates climate information, hydrological models, river-network data, and machine learning methods to improve prediction of water-related hazards from local catchments to global river systems.
